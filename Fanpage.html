Bio

Exo is a Korean Pop boy band originally consisting of 12 members upon debut in 2012, although currently there are 9 remaining members. EXO debuted under their company S.M. Entertainment (SME) with the hexagonal logo that encapsulates their letters E-X-O, a brand later to be made into a light-stick (see bottom image) used for cheering at concerts. In 2012, the group was divided into a Korean unit, Exo-K (K for Korean), and a Chinese Unit, Exo-M for (M for Mandarin). The Korean subunit was comprised of leader Suho, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, D.O., Kai, and Sehun while the Chinese unit included leader Kris Wu, Xiumin, Lay, Luhan, Chen, and Tao.

Although the exact reasons for departure were not officially made public, it is assumed that the three Chinese members Kris Wu, Luhan, and Tao each had issues with SME in regard to unfair treatment and pay due to their race. The still-active, fourth and final Chinese member, Lay, currently leads solo productions in China whilst his Korean counterparts continue to promote solo ventures in Korea under the group's brand. Some Korean members, such as D.O. and Xiumin, are currently serving in the Korean military and are absent from promotions.

The band might seem to be reaching stagnation in terms of their career what with the members so split between solo activities. However, this is far from the case. The members are all still very active in their own right. Some produce solo albums and others take on modeling and product promotion activities, while others star in films and Korean dramas. Even with their individual activities temporarily drawing them away from a group come back, EXO still fits itself in as a household name, ranking amongst the top K-pop groups in South Korea in their 8-year, trailblazing career. Naturally so, as they release new, improved songs each year and continuously sell out entire stadiums on their tours across numerous countries spanning from Asia, Europe, and the Americas.
